The Three Types of Trees

As you probably know, there are many different types of animals grouped in a certain way. Similarly, there are many tpyes of plants grouped into three main types. These are conifers, broadleaves, and palms.

Conifers

Conifer is a word derived Latin, literally meaning the one that bears cones. ( conus =cone, ferre =bearing) The total number of species of conifers is relatively small, but still, conifers have a significant importance in our world. Most conifers in the northern region are in a thin pyramidal shape with branches pointing down, which help them shed snow in the winter. The earliest conifers in the fossil record date about 300 million years ago, possibly arising from Cordaites, a seed-bearing plant with cone-like fertile structures. This plant resembled the modern monkey puzzle. Also, it is really easy for you to determine whether a tree is a conifer or not. To determine this, you should look for three main things. First of all, that the leaves are scaly,needle-like, and usually pointed. Next that the fruit is like a cone, and is scaly, and in most cases woody, though in some cases it can be fleshy. Also, the last, but definitely not the last is that most of the conifers are evergreen, meaning that they have leaves that they do not shed during the winter. Usually, if a tree meets two or more of these criteria, the tree is probably a conifer. But, if you are not sure, it is always better to make sure.

Broadleaf

A broad leaved tree is any tree, like the name suggests, which has broad leaves. Also, many people get confused in thinking that there is no difference between the word deciduous, and the word broad leaved. Thankfully, you will see the difference. A broadleaved tree is any tree that has broad leaves, or to be specific, leaves that are not needle-like. Most broadleaves, however, are deciduous, meaning that they shed their leaves in the fall, therefore, having no leaves in the winter. This helps the tree, as the snow can get caught in the leaves, and can break, or even topple a tree. But, when the leaves are not on the tree, the snow can easily fall through the gaps. But don't just think that all broadleaves are deciduous. An example of this is the osage orange, or maclura pomifera, which sheds its leaves in the winter. There are some broadleaved trees that can also be evergreen, such as the live oak. These trees can sustain the winter even with their leaves. Therefore, they usually have leathery, tough leaves that the delicate, paper-like leaves that many trees have. Also, it is really easy for you to determine whether a tree is a broadleaf or not. To determine this, you should look for three main things. First of all, that the leaves are broad, and thin. Next that the fruit is either a nut, or a fleshy fruit, both, usually inclosed in a husk or skin. Also, the last, but definitely not the last is that most of the broadleaves are deciduous, meaning that they have leaves that they shed during the winter. Usually, if a tree meets two or more of these criteria, the tree is probably a broadleaf. But, if you are not sure, it is always better to make sure.

Palms

A palm is a plant that is evergreen, meaning that it does not shed its leaves in the winter, but unlike the conifers, there are no exceptions. Also, the unlike the conifers which mostly grow in the relatively cooler regions, all of the palms grow very near the equatorial region. These monocotyledons, or trees with a single rudimentary leaf, are usually with a simple stem with a terminal crown of leaves that are usually fan-like, or feather-like in shape. Also, these leaves are pinnate,or palmately cleft leaves. Palms are of the genre Arecaceae or Palmae. Also, it is really easy for you to determine whether a tree is a palm or not. To determine this, you should look for three main things. First of all, that the leaves are feather-like, or fan-like, and usually pointed. Next that the it has a single stemless trunk with one crown at the top. Also, the last, but definitely not the last is that all palms are evergreen, meaning that they have leaves that they do not shed. This is as the palms usually grow in the equatorial, or tropical zone. Usually, if a tree meets two or more of these criteria, the tree is probably a palm. But, if you are not sure, it is always better to make sure.
